01

Atacama Desert, Chile

6,112 reviews

For an out-of-this-world adventure, look no further than the Atacama Desert in western Chile. With jagged ravines, billowing geysers and arid salt flats, there’s an extra-terrestrial quality to its landscapes that NASA once harnessed to field test Martian rovers.....As one of the driest places on the planet, there’s very little in the way of human development surrounding its barren plains. That said, the resulting lack of light pollution makes the sky perfect for stargazing

This temperate grasslands, savannas, and shrublands ecoregion of the Palearctic realm forms a large crescent around the Gobi Desert, extending across central and eastern Mongolia into the eastern portion of Inner Mongolia and eastern and central Manchuria, and then southwest across the North China Plain....Genghis Khan with a ride on a Mongol horse and a heartly bowl od airag.

Papua New Guinea is THE destination for a birding, photography or artefact collector, and also a challenge for adventurous travellers to walk the famous World War 2 Kokoda Track.....Sepik River past rugged jungle and traditional villages, overnighting in eco lodges along the way.

Myeik Archipelago,Myanmar

Myeik (also known as Mergui Archipelago) is a group of more than 800 pristine islands of varying sizes located in the Southern region of Myanmar on the Andaman Sea.These untouched islands are renowed for their pristine white sand beaches and diverse marine life.....These is heaven for nature lovers and those looking for private beaach and islands at their holidays

Northern Territory,Australia

4,432 reviews

With shifting heat,sun baked landscapes and crocodile-infested waterways,the northean territory is australia at its rawest. The waterfront city of Dawn is fateway to the ourback, and from here you can adventure deep into the Read center in the search of world's most beautiful places....The NT covers 1,349,129 square kilometres (520,902 sq mi), making it the third-largest Australian federal division, and the 11th-largest country subdivision in the world.

Ciudad Perdida(Lost City),Columbia

7,899 reviews

Deep in the jungle-swathed mountains of sierra Nevada lies La Ciuded Perdida,Colombia's fabled ruined city dubbed "the new Machu Picchu".....Ciudad Perdida (Spanish for "lost city"; also known as Teyuna and Buritaca-200[1]) is the archaeological site of an ancient city in the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta of Colombia, within the jurisdiction of the city of Santa Marta.

07

The Galapagos Islands,Ecuador

8,355 reviews

The unique wildlife here has evolved to be fearless,stemming from the lack of predators on the volcanic isles,so you can easily snorkel with marine iguanas or share the rocky outcrops with the group of giant tortoises.....The Galápagos are famous for their large number of endemic species, which were studied by Charles Darwin in the 1830s and inspired his theory of evolution by means of natural selection.

08

The Dolomites,Italy

12,000 reviews

The Dolomites in the northean Italy have one of the highest concentrations of via ferrata in the world,giving hikers access to the craggy peaks without the need of high-tech equipmentsThe steel cables,ladders and suspended bridges are free to use.....The Dolomites are renowned for skiing in the winter months and mountain climbing, hiking, cycling and BASE jumping, as well as paragliding and hang gliding in summer and late spring/early autumn.[8][9] Free climbing has been a tradition in the Dolomites.

09

Hemis National Park,India

reviews

Hemis National Park is a high-elevation national park in Ladakh, India. Globally famous for its snow leopards, it is believed to have the highest density of them in any protected area in the world.The park is home to a number of species of endangered mammals, including the snow leopard.....The park offers a number of routes for trekking from mid-June to mid-October; some of these trekking routes are among the most popular in Ladakh. This includes the Markha valley trek and the trek from Spituk to Stok over the Ganda La pass.The Hemis National Park is also famous for mountaineering expeditions.

10

Antarctica

3,467 reviews

Antarctica is, on average, the coldest, driest, and windiest of the continents, and it has the highest average elevation. It is mainly a polar desert, with annual precipitation of over 200 mm (8 in) along the coast and far less inland. About 70% of the world's freshwater reserves are frozen in Antarctica.....While historic tales of polar exploration are fraught with shipwrecks and disasters,Antartica is rapidly emerging as a popular adventure travel destination.
